# Dating Strategy Research: Online Dating Best Practices for Clap Cheeks AI Coaching

**Researched:** 2026-03-01
**Domain:** Online dating optimization — Tinder, Bumble, Hinge
**Purpose:** Inform AI coaching engine, conversation manager, and profile optimizer for Clap Cheeks

---

## Executive Summary

Online dating in 2024-2025 is a deeply asymmetric game. Men face match rates of 2-3%, women face match rates of 30%+. The top 10% of male profiles get the vast majority of matches — not because they're better looking, but because they've optimized every layer of the funnel: profile, swiping strategy, openers, and conversation conversion.

The platforms have evolved significantly. Tinder moved beyond ELO (though desirability scoring persists in evolved form). Bumble introduced "Opening Moves" in 2024, removing the hard requirement for women to message first. Hinge uses a Nobel-prize-winning Gale-Shapley algorithm for "Most Compatible" daily recommendations. Each platform has a distinct user intent: Tinder skews casual, Hinge skews relationship-oriented, Bumble sits in the middle.

**85% of users don't complete their profile.** Simply having a full, optimized profile puts someone in the top 15% before they send a single message.

## 1. Profile Optimization

### 1.1 Photos: The Dominant Lever

Photos drive 90%+ of swipe decisions. Users make the initial swiping decision in **0.3 seconds** when viewing the primary photo. Everything else is secondary.

**Key photo statistics:**
- High-quality photos are **21x more likely** to result in a date vs. low-quality ones
- Profiles with a full-body shot see a **203% increase** in matches
- Profiles with 4-6 varied, high-quality photos get **38% more matches** than those with 1-3
- Smiling photos get **14% more right swipes** than serious/moody expressions
- Optimal photo count: **6-8 photos** (fewer signals low effort, more signals overthinking)

**Photo order — recommended sequence:**
1. **Primary photo:** Clear face, smiling, high quality, natural light. Eye contact with camera. Solo shot. This is the make-or-break moment.
2. **Full-body shot:** Casual, in a real context (not a gym selfie). Shows physique without being try-hard.
3. **Social context / group shot:** Shows you have friends, are likeable. Keep it clear which person you are.
4. **Activity / hobby photo:** In your element doing something you love — hiking, cooking, playing music, at a game. Gives conversation hooks.
5. **Travel or interesting location:** Signals adventurousness. Not mandatory but strong when available.
6. **Candid / genuine moment:** Laughing at a party, caught mid-action. Humanizes you.

**Photo types that consistently underperform:**
- Sunglasses in every photo (hides face, reads as hiding something)
- Bathroom mirror selfies
- Blurry or dark photos
- Group photos as the primary photo (which one are you?)
- Photos with ex cropped out (visible arm, obvious crop)
- Shirtless as the primary photo (works better as photo 3-5 if physique warrants it)

**The pet photo effect:** Photos with dogs get significantly more right swipes. If you have a dog, use it. If you don't, borrow one.

### 1.2 Bio Strategy by Platform

**Tinder (500 character limit — use it):**
- Tinder bios are a blank canvas. Most people write nothing or 2 sentences.
- Optimal length: 150-400 characters — enough to show personality, short enough to read instantly
- Tone: Playful, confident, specific. Avoid lists of generic adjectives ("adventurous," "loves to laugh")
- Give one concrete hook people can message about
- End with a soft call-to-action or question to lower the barrier to opening
- Example structure: Quirky self-description + one specific interest or fact + implicit invitation to chat
- Avoid: negativity ("not here for hookups"), demands, checklists of what you want in a partner

**Bumble:**
- Similar to Tinder — photo-first with supporting bio
- Slightly more relationship-oriented user base, so slightly more depth is rewarded
- Women: bio matters less because they control who sees them via swiping; focus on photos
- Men: bio should give women something to anchor an Opening Move response to

**Hinge (prompt-based, no traditional bio):**
- Three prompts replace the bio. This is where Hinge users spend creative energy.
- Prompts are the primary conversation starter — people like and comment on prompt answers, not photos
- Prompt selection matters: choose prompts that invite responses, not ones with closed answers
- Answer length sweet spot: **100-200 characters** — gets highest response rates
- High-engagement prompt answer formulas:
  - **Humor trigger:** Oddly specific, unexpected answer. "My most irrational fear: clapping-activated lights"
  - **Relatability trigger:** Something universal expressed in a specific way
  - **Curiosity trigger:** Answer that raises a question in the reader's mind, compels them to ask
  - **Flirt trigger:** Something playful with mild innuendo or chemistry
  - **Emotional depth trigger:** Genuine, slightly vulnerable answer that shows self-awareness

**High-performing Hinge prompts (that invite engagement):**
- "The most spontaneous thing I've done..." (storytelling hook)
- "I'm looking for..." (direct, shows intent — best answered with something specific + slightly funny)
- "Dating me is like..." (creative framing, infinite possible answers)
- "My simple pleasures..." (easy to relate to, shows personality)
- "Two truths and a lie..." (built-in game, invites guessing)
- "We'll get along if..." (sets expectations, filters in right people)

**Prompts to avoid on Hinge:**
- "I go crazy for..." (cliché)
- "My greatest strength is..." (feels like a job interview)
- Anything with a one-word answer like "pizza"

### 1.3 Video Prompts (Hinge)

Hinge profiles with video prompts receive **50% more engagement**. This is underutilized. Any Hinge coaching should push users toward adding a video prompt.

## 2. Swiping Strategy

### 2.1 Tinder Algorithm (Post-ELO)

Tinder officially retired the ELO score framing but the underlying desirability-scoring logic persists. The current system evaluates:

- **Match-to-like ratio:** If you match 8 out of 10 likes sent, the algorithm rewards you. If you match fewer than 3 out of 10, your score drops. Swiping right indiscriminately tanks this ratio.
- **Engagement quality:** How often you message matches, response rates, conversation depth
- **Recency:** Active users get boosted
- **Profile completeness:** Complete profiles rank higher

**Optimal like ratio:** Aim for 20-30% right swipes. The algorithm interprets selectivity as a signal of quality. Swiping right on everything signals low standards and suppresses your profile visibility.

**Key insight for AI:** An AI that swipes right on everyone is actively harming the user's algorithmic standing. The AI must be selective.

### 2.2 Bumble Algorithm

Bumble's algorithm factors:
- **Activity level:** Active users shown more frequently. Log in daily.
- **Messaging behavior:** Initiating conversations (for women) within 24 hours boosts ranking. Men who respond promptly to Opening Moves are also rewarded.
- **Match expiry:** Letting matches expire without messaging is a negative signal.
- **Selectivity:** Same as Tinder — indiscriminate right-swiping reduces visibility.

**2024 change:** Bumble introduced "Opening Moves" — women set a pre-written prompt question, men respond to it. This removes the hard 24-hour women-must-message-first rule. Both parties can now initiate, but the Opening Move frame is still set by whoever uses it.

### 2.3 Hinge Algorithm

Hinge uses the **Gale-Shapley stable matching algorithm** combined with machine learning. Key mechanics:
- "Most Compatible" shows one person per day. Both parties see each other simultaneously. Disappears in 24 hours.
- People are **8x more likely to date their Most Compatible match** than regular matches
- Algorithm improves over time as it learns from your like/pass behavior
- Completing prompts thoroughly and honestly helps the algorithm categorize you accurately
- Deep-learning updates in 2025 contributed to a **double-digit increase in matches overall**

**To maximize Most Compatible:**
- Like and pass consistently (teaches the algorithm your taste)
- Complete all prompts honestly
- Engage with likes you receive promptly
- Set dealbreakers accurately — they filter what gets shown to you

### 2.4 Timing: When to Swipe

**Peak hours (highest chance of real-time matches and conversations):**
- **Sunday 7-10 PM** — highest traffic of the week across all platforms
- **Thursday 7-10 PM** — second highest
- **Wednesday 8 PM** — mid-week spike, less competition than weekend
- First Sunday of January ("Dating Sunday") — reliably off-the-charts activity

**Tinder Boost timing:** Use boosts at **9 PM on Sunday** for maximum exposure. Second best: Thursday at 9 PM. Boosts during off-peak hours are wasted.

**Bad weather effect:** Rain, snow, and storms increase app activity by ~10%. Note seasonally relevant.

### 2.5 Burst vs. Steady Swiping

No strong data on burst vs. steady. However, consistent daily activity outperforms irregular binge sessions. The algorithms favor users who log in regularly. Recommended: 15-20 minutes of active swiping during peak evening hours rather than 2-hour Sunday sessions.

## 3. Opening Messages

### 3.1 What the Data Says

**Gender baselines:**
- Women: ~50% response rate to initial messages
- Men: ~21% response rate to initial messages
- Men need to send 18 messages to women their own age to be 50% certain of getting one response

**What moves the needle for men:**
- Including "you mention" in the first message raises response likelihood by **50%**
- Phrases with 40%+ reply rates: "curious what," "your name" (using their name), "noticed that," "good taste"
- GIF-accompanied messages get **30% more responses** and conversations last **2x as long**
- Funny messages: women were **12% more likely** to reply to funny openers than earnest ones (Irrational Labs, N=1,227)
- Question-based openers: **30% higher response rate** than statements

**Time sensitivity:**
- For men on Hinge: If no response in first **6 hours**, response likelihood drops 25%
- Women are more patient (5% drop in the same window)
- Matches that go cold after 24-48 hours rarely recover

### 3.2 Opener Frameworks That Work

**Formula 1: Specific observation + question**
Comment on one specific detail in their profile (not generic like "cute smile") + ask a follow-up question.
- "Your answer about [oddly specific Hinge prompt] made me laugh — is that actually true or are you testing people?"
- "I see you went to [place in travel photo] — what's the one thing you'd do differently on that trip?"

**Formula 2: Playful humor + easy response**
Short, witty, gives them something to riff on. The Irrational Labs winning message: "Did we have class together? I could've sworn we had chemistry."
- Low stakes, funny, invites a playful back-and-forth without requiring effort to respond

**Formula 3: GIF + question**
Send a GIF that relates to something in their profile or is just funny/on-brand for you, followed by a short question. GIFs do the heavy emotional lifting.

**Formula 4: Direct and confident (works better for men)**
"You seem like trouble. I'm [name] — what's your deal?"
Polarizing but filters for people who appreciate directness.

**What doesn't work:**
- "Hey" / "Hi" / "What's up" (treated as major red flag by most users)
- Generic compliments about looks ("you're gorgeous")
- Long paragraphs in the first message (overwhelming)
- Leading with questions about where someone went to school or what they do

**Opener length:** 1-2 sentences is optimal. Under 20 words for the text component.

### 3.3 Platform-Specific Opening Strategy

**Tinder:** You initiate from scratch. Use humor + profile-specific observation formula. GIF openers are strong here.

**Bumble:**
- Women using Opening Moves: Pre-set a good question that's slightly playful. "What's your signature dish?" works better than "What are you looking for?" The latter feels like an interview.
- Men responding to Opening Moves: Give a specific, slightly funny answer + flip a question back at them. Don't just answer the question.
- Women messaging first without Opening Moves: "Comment + question" formula — comment on something specific in his profile, then ask a question.

**Hinge:** Matches happen when someone likes a photo or prompt answer. The opener should reference what they liked or what you liked. If they liked your prompt answer, acknowledge it; if you liked their photo/prompt, lead with that specific reference.
- Hinge also lets you send a comment when liking — this IS the opener. Use it. A thoughtful comment on a prompt when liking dramatically increases match rate.

## 4. Conversation to Date Conversion

### 4.1 The Conversion Arc

The optimal conversation arc has three phases:

1. **Rapport (messages 1-5):** Playful banter, find common ground, demonstrate personality. Avoid interview-mode questions. Use statements and observations as much as questions.

2. **Qualification (messages 5-10):** Escalate to slightly more personal territory. Find out about their life without interrogating. Express genuine curiosity. Let them qualify themselves to you — make them feel like they're convincing YOU.

3. **Date ask (messages 8-15):** Don't let it drag. The longer the conversation without a date being set, the more it becomes a pen-pal situation. Propose the date specifically.

### 4.2 When to Ask for a Date / Number

**Key counterintuitive finding:** Asking for a phone number before suggesting a date has a **60% chance the date won't happen**. Skip the number exchange and propose the date directly on the app.

**Recommended approach:**
- Skip "can I get your number?" entirely
- Instead: "We should grab [drinks/coffee/activity] — are you free [specific day] or [specific day]?"
- Giving two specific day options is better than open-ended "sometime" — reduces cognitive load, easier to say yes
- Best timing: propose the date when they're clearly engaged and investing in the conversation

**Message count benchmarks:**
- 5-10 messages before proposing the date is the consensus sweet spot
- 5-7 days of messaging before exchanging numbers (if number exchange is needed)
- Any longer than 2 weeks without setting a date = pen-pal territory, dramatically lower conversion

### 4.3 How to Ask for the Date

**Direct frame (most effective):**
"You seem fun — let's grab drinks. Thursday or Saturday work for you?"

**The "we should" frame:**
"We should grab coffee this week — does [day] work?"

**Activity invite (disarming, lower stakes):**
"There's a [farmers market / outdoor concert / art thing] on [day] — want to check it out?"

**What doesn't work:**
- "Maybe we could hang out sometime?" (vague = usually no)
- Asking permission-style: "Would you want to maybe...?" (hedging signals low confidence)
- Suggesting dinner for the first date (too much commitment, too long, too expensive)

### 4.4 Handling Slow Responders and Dead Conversations

**Re-engagement after going cold (24-72 hours of silence):**
- Don't double-text immediately — wait 24-48 hours
- Send a re-engagement message that's low-stakes and value-adding (funny observation, meme, reference to something they mentioned)
- Don't guilt-trip or reference that they didn't respond
- Limit re-engagement attempts to 1-2 max. If still no response, move on.

**After a flake (they cancel a planned date):**
- Stay nonchalant. "No worries, I'll catch you another time" is the correct tone
- Don't show hurt or demand explanation
- Wait 3 days before re-engaging to reschedule
- If they reschedule with a specific alternative time: genuine interest. If they say "I'll let you know" or "maybe next week" — low interest, don't push
- Second flake: move on. Two flakes is a pattern.

**Rule of thumb for re-engagement:** One follow-up per dead conversation. Two maximum. After that, let it go. Energy is better spent on new leads.

## 5. Managing Multiple Conversations ("The Roster")

### 5.1 The Volume Reality

Top-performing male users are often managing 10-20+ conversations at once. Women managing the same. The ethical and practical questions are real — here's the research consensus:

**Ideal simultaneous conversation count:** Experts suggest 3 genuine conversations at once for meaningful connection. Practically, power users manage up to 20 but most conversations are low-engagement.

**The quality-quantity tradeoff:** The more conversations running simultaneously, the less you pick up on nuance from each individual. Past ~9 active conversations, personalization degrades significantly.

**Industry norm on disclosure:** Until exclusivity is explicitly discussed, it's generally accepted in dating culture that both parties are talking to multiple people. No obligation to disclose this proactively. The conversation about exclusivity should happen before becoming physically exclusive.

### 5.2 Systems for Managing at Scale

**Tracking detail:**
- Use notes (phone notes app, CRM-style) to log key facts about each match: name, job, city context, a memorable detail from early conversation, date of last message
- Before resuming a cold conversation, review notes to personalize re-engagement
- Flag: when you've made a date plan, move them to a "confirmed" list

**Prioritization framework:**
- Tier 1: Active conversation + date set — invest heavily, be responsive
- Tier 2: Active conversation, good momentum — respond same-day
- Tier 3: Slow/stalled — respond when convenient, don't chase
- Tier 4: Cold (3+ days, no response) — re-engage once then archive

**When to let a conversation go cold:**
- Monosyllabic responses without reciprocal questions after 3+ exchanges
- She stops asking questions entirely
- 3+ days of silence without explanation
- Pattern of slow responses + minimal effort

### 5.3 Personalization at Scale

The core skill is **remembering specifics and referencing them later**. Callbacks to earlier conversation details are the single most reliable way to signal genuine interest.

- "You mentioned you were going to [place] this weekend — how was it?"
- "Didn't you say you had that thing with your [friend/family] this weekend?"

**AI opportunity here is massive:** An AI system that maintains conversation state and surfaces callbacks at the right moment is 10x more effective than a human doing it manually across 15+ conversations.

## 6. Scheduling & Date Logistics

### 6.1 Date Scheduling Principles

**Never use Calendly for first dates.** This is a dealbreaker for most people. It feels corporate, transactional, and sends the message that you treat dating like a business appointment. Use it for nothing dating-related.

**The two-option day proposal** (as noted in section 4): "Thursday or Saturday?" is better than "when are you free?" Reduces cognitive load and shows you've thought about it.

**Ideal day for first dates:**
- Weeknight (Tuesday-Thursday) is actually preferred by many — less pressure than a "big" weekend date, easier to keep it to 1-2 hours if the vibe is off
- Saturday/Sunday afternoons work well for casual coffee/activity dates
- Avoid Friday nights for a first date — feels like a high-stakes "official date" night to many people, raises expectations

**Suggested lead time:** 2-4 days out. Proposing same-day feels desperate. More than 5 days out risks losing momentum.

### 6.2 First Date Venue Strategy

**Coffee:**
- Low stakes, casual, easy to exit
- Lower barrier to say yes (low cost, daytime, public)
- Can extend easily if it's going well
- Downside: slightly platonic energy, less romantically charged
- Best for: when match is slightly hesitant or when you want to keep it ultra-low-pressure

**Drinks (bar):**
- More romantic/charged environment than coffee
- Evening timing sets a better mood
- Slightly higher barrier (cost, commitment of evening)
- Alcohol reduces anxiety — and occasionally judgment
- Best for: building romantic tension, when there's already good chemistry established in conversation

**Activity-based:**
- Mini golf, bowling, escape room, museum, farmers market
- Adrenaline activities (rock climbing, axe throwing) are documented to increase attraction via misattribution of arousal
- Great for people who find one-on-one conversation uncomfortable in the early stages
- Downside: can limit conversation depth

**Consensus recommendation:** Drinks at a good bar. Casual enough to be low-stakes, romantic enough to create the right mood. Keep it 1-1.5 hours. Have a second venue option nearby if it's going well ("I know a great spot around the corner").

**What to avoid:** Dinner for a first date. Too long, too expensive, too formal, creates artificial intimacy pressure.

### 6.3 Follow-Up After a Good First Date

**Same-night text:** "I had a great time — let's do it again soon" within 1-2 hours of the date. Shows confidence and intent.

**Second date ask:** Within 24-48 hours of the first date. Don't wait 3-5 days playing games. The "3-day rule" is dead. People who play it now read as unsure or uninterested.

**Second date venue:** Slightly more intentional than the first — dinner, cooking together, something with more investment. You're building on established connection.

## 7. Platform-Specific Meta

### 7.1 Tinder

**Is Tinder Gold worth it?**
- Gold: Passport, unlimited likes, see who liked you, rewind — solid value if you're actively dating and in a city
- The "see who liked you" feature lets you focus your right swipes on people already interested, dramatically improving match-to-like ratio
- Verdict: Worth it for active users in metro areas. Not worth it in low-density locations.

**Boost:**
- Gives 30x profile visibility for 30 minutes
- Best time: Sunday 9 PM, Thursday 9 PM
- Won't fix a bad profile. Boost a fully optimized profile only.
- Comes with 1 free boost/month on Gold

**Super Likes (now called Super Wants):**
- SwipeStats data: Top 1% of Super Like users achieved only 1.94% match rate — **worse** than average male performance without Super Likes
- Verdict: Not worth money. The data suggests they may even hurt if used on profiles you're unlikely to match with, as the low match rate signals hurt your algorithmic standing.

**Platform intent:** Tinder skews most casual. Highest user volume (~75M monthly active users). Best for cities with large user base.

### 7.2 Bumble

**2024 Changes:**
- Opening Moves launched: women can pre-set a question prompt; men respond, effectively letting both parties initiate
- Removed the hard requirement for women to message first (though the 24-hour window structure remains for those who don't use Opening Moves)

**SuperSwipe:**
- Notifies match that you super-swiped before they decide
- Better than Tinder Super Likes for scarcity signaling — receiving a signal of effort increases acceptance
- Verdict: Use sparingly on profiles you're genuinely very interested in. Not a volume strategy.

**Spotlight:**
- Boosts you to the top of the stack for 30 minutes
- More effective spend than SuperSwipes if you're buying premium features
- Best used during peak evening hours

**BFF Mode:** Not relevant to the dating coaching use case.

**Platform intent:** Bumble positions as more relationship-oriented than Tinder. Women tend to be slightly more serious about intent. Good middle ground.

### 7.3 Hinge

**Most Compatible:**
- Use it every day. 8x higher date rate than regular matches. This is the most underutilized feature on any dating app.
- Like the Most Compatible suggestion within 24 hours before it disappears
- If you both like each other from Most Compatible, treat it as a warm lead — open with high energy

**Rose (1 free per week, replenishes Sunday):**
- Equivalent to Tinder Super Like but with better optics (Hinge's branding is warmer)
- Can purchase additional Roses
- Your weekly free Rose should always be used — on someone genuinely compelling, not wasted
- Roses bypass the queue — if they have a lot of likes, your Rose goes to the top

**Hinge Preferred / Hinge+:**
- Unlimited likes (free is capped)
- See everyone who liked you (massive efficiency gain)
- Advanced filters (height, dealbreakers)
- Worth it for active users — same logic as Tinder Gold

**Platform intent:** Hinge is explicitly "designed to be deleted" — relationship-oriented. User base skews slightly more educated and relationship-minded. Lower volume than Tinder but much higher quality signal in prompts and conversations.

**Which platform for what:**
| Goal | Best Platform |
|------|--------------|
| Casual / hookups | Tinder |
| Relationships | Hinge |
| Middle ground / women making first move | Bumble |
| 35+ serious | Match, eHarmony |
| Queer dating | HER, Grindr, also Hinge and Bumble |

### 8.1 Baseline Metrics

| Metric | Men | Women |
|--------|-----|-------|
| Average match rate | 2-3% (Tinder avg: 2.63%) | 30-50% |
| Matches needed for 1 date | ~100+ matches | ~10 matches |
| Initial message response rate | ~21% | ~50% |

| Avg right swipes needed for 1 match (men) | ~38 profiles | ~3 profiles |

**Source:** SwipeStats (N=3,700+ profiles), Irrational Labs, Pew Research Center

### 8.2 Conversion Funnel (Men on Tinder, approximate)

```
1,000 right swipes
→ ~26 matches (2.6% match rate)
→ ~5-6 responses to opening messages (21% response rate)
→ ~1-2 conversations reaching date-ask stage
→ ~0.5-1 actual dates
```

This is why top users win through optimization at every stage — not just volume.

### 8.3 What Separates Top 10% Users

1. **Profile is fully complete.** 85% of users don't complete their profile. A full, well-optimized profile instantly puts you in the top 15%.

2. **6+ high-quality photos.** Most profiles have 1-3 mediocre photos.

3. **Selective swiping.** Top performers maintain high match-to-like ratio by swiping selectively, keeping the algorithm rewarding them.

4. **Personalized openers.** Reference something specific in the profile. Most people send "hey."

5. **Move to date quickly.** Top performers propose dates within 5-10 messages. Average users never propose.

6. **Re-engagement strategy.** They follow up on cold matches with a relevant, low-stakes message.

7. **Daily activity during peak hours.** They're active when it counts.

8. **Use of premium features strategically.** Gold/Preferred for the efficiency ("see who likes you" feature).

### 8.4 Academic Research

- **Science Advances (Michigan study):** Men tend to pursue women 25% more desirable than themselves on average. Women with a simple, direct first message that references something specific outperform generic openers by a wide margin.

- **OkCupid internal data:** Messages that use the recipient's name and reference a specific interest have a 40%+ reply rate vs. <10% for generic greetings.

- **Pew Research (2023):** 30% of U.S. adults have used a dating app. Satisfaction varies widely — most find the experience overall negative, which is a product opportunity.

## 9. Key Insights for AI Coaching

This section synthesizes everything above into what the Clap Cheeks AI coaching engine should prioritize.

### 9.1 The Hierarchy of Leverage

The following is ranked by ROI (impact per unit of effort):

1. **Profile photo quality** — The single highest-leverage intervention. One professional or high-quality photo shoot can 10x-20x matches. The AI coach should lead with this.

2. **Profile completeness** — 85% of users don't complete their profile. Getting a user to fill out all prompts, add 6+ photos, and write a compelling bio moves them to top 15% without any other change.

3. **Selective swiping** — Counterintuitively, an AI that swipes right on fewer profiles will get better results for the user algorithmically. The AI must enforce selectivity.

4. **Opener personalization** — Reference something specific. Include a question. Use humor. These three factors each independently move response rates significantly.

5. **Speed to date** — The #1 conversion killer is conversations that drag without a date being proposed. AI should flag when it's time to move to the date ask (typically after 5-10 messages of good engagement).

6. **Timing** — Sunday 7-10 PM for swiping and messaging. Simple win that most users don't optimize.

7. **Platform selection** — Match the user to the right platform for their goal.

### 9.2 The AI Coach's Core Curriculum

**Module 1: Profile Audit**
- Evaluate all photos (quality, order, types present)
- Check for the full-body shot (203% match increase if missing — this is the single highest-ROI addition)
- Audit Hinge prompts for engagement triggers (humor, curiosity, specificity)
- Audit Tinder bio for specificity and hooks
- Score overall and prescribe specific changes

**Module 2: Swipe Strategy**
- Train users to swipe selectively (20-30% right swipe target)
- Educate on peak timing
- Advise on which platform to prioritize based on stated goals

**Module 3: Opener Coaching**
- Template library: specific-observation + question, GIF + question, playful humor formula
- Coach users to reference specific profile elements
- Flag generic openers ("hey," compliments on looks alone) and suggest rewrites
- Real-time feedback on draft messages

**Module 4: Conversation Management**
- Track conversation state per match (last message, days since last message, key facts)
- Surface callback opportunities ("You mentioned last week she was going hiking — good re-engagement hook")
- Flag when conversation is ready for date-ask (engagement level + message count signals)
- Suggest date-ask phrasing based on conversation context
- Manage re-engagement for cold conversations

**Module 5: Date Logistics**
- Prescribe venue type based on match's expressed personality and vibe
- Provide day/time suggestions optimized for success
- Follow-up coaching after dates (what to say, when to say it, how to ask for date 2)

**Module 6: Roster Management**
- Prioritization dashboard: active leads vs cold leads
- Reminder system to re-engage at optimal timing
- Note-taking and recall: surface key facts before resuming conversations

### 9.3 Platform-Specific AI Tactics

**For Tinder:**
- Enforce selective swiping (track and coach like ratio)
- Focus opener coaching on humor and specificity
- Boost timing alerts (Sunday/Thursday 9 PM)
- "See who liked you" (Gold feature) is the single most efficient use of premium

**For Bumble:**
- Coach women on Opening Move selection (playful, specific, easy to answer)
- Coach men on responding to Opening Moves (answer + flip a question back)
- Flag the 24-hour expiry window on matches — messaging urgency coaching
- Spotlight > SuperSwipe for premium spend

**For Hinge:**
- Most Compatible is the highest-value daily action — AI should remind users to check it and like/pass within 24 hours
- Prompt answer optimization is the highest-leverage profile intervention (Hinge conversations start here)
- Rose management: track free weekly Rose, ensure it's used before Sunday replenishment
- Coach on commenting when liking (the Hinge opener mechanism)

### 9.4 The Clap Cheeks Moat

The data reveals a massive opportunity: most users fail not because they lack attraction or personality, but because they don't know the meta. They:
- Post mediocre photos in the wrong order
- Swipe right on everything and get algorithmically penalized
- Send "hey" and wait
- Let conversations stall without proposing dates
- Don't know when or how to re-engage

Clap Cheeks AI can close every single one of these gaps systematically. The coaching value isn't in being charismatic for the user — it's in removing the friction points that convert interested matches into ignored conversations into missed dates.

**The product insight:** Users don't need AI to be charming. They need AI to be consistent, systematic, and informed in ways that humans naturally aren't when juggling modern dating at scale.
